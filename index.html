Welcome, Dear Gentle Reader,

Hon’ble Lady Janhvi presents: A Most Delightful Account of the Ton And while I claim no kinship to Lady Whistledown, I daresay I observe just as keenly… But of course, I’ve come bearing not just observations, but opinions—rather strong ones, if I may be so bold—regarding my beloved show, Bridgerton. Bridgerton is, at its heart, an American alternate history Regency romance—yes, dear reader, alternate, not inaccurate. (Alternate history, or allohistory, for those inclined toward scholarly precision, is a subgenre of speculative fiction in which one or more historical events occur differently than in the actual timeline. Consider it history... with a little more imagination and far better wardrobe. Created by Chris Van Dusen for Netflix, and based on Julia Quinn’s delectable book series (and let’s be honest—as if a man could dream up such intricately laced corsets and smoldering eye contact without some help from the female gaze... lovely attempt though, Mr. Van Dusen), Bridgerton marked the first scripted production from Shondaland for the platform. And my, what a debut it was—scandal, symphonies, and sighs included.
